Pair Programming

Slides are at pair.josephabell.co.uk

Wikipedia says:

Pair programming is a software development technique in which two programmers work together at one workstation. One programmer writes code while the other reviews each line of code as it is typed in. The two programmers switch roles frequently.

Benefits (From MIT)

  • You talk to people!
  • You gain confidence in your solutions.
  • You are much less likely to overengineer a solution to a problem.
  • Collaborators can often perform tasks that are too challenging for one to do alone.
  • Problems are removed before they ever enter the codebase.
